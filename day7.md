Biggest Mistakes I Should Avoid
Over-allocating Max Effort on Chat Tasks: Do not run your mock interview simulations or quick proofreads on Max Effort. It will slow down the response time significantly and consume massive token budgets unnecessarily on tasks that don't benefit from heavy reasoning.

Using Vague Prompts for Resume Building: Claude Sonnet is an exceptional professional writer, but it cannot read your mind. Avoid general prompts like "Make my resume look good." Instead, feed it context: the target job description, your raw experience data, and the high-effort setting.

Fearing the "Thought Blocks": When using higher effort settings, you will see Claude's internal thinking process happen live on screen. Do not be intimidated by this text wall; it's a great tool to visually audit how the AI reached its business or career conclusions.

Final Recommendation
If you could use only ONE model and ONE effort level for 90% of your current work, use Claude Sonnet on High Effort.

High Effort unlocks Sonnet's deep, adaptive reasoning engine, ensuring your resumes read like they were written by an elite executive coach and your learning pathways are built with genuine conceptual depth.
